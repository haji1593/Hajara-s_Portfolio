## The question

For a utility like the **Electricity Company of Ghana**, performance isn't one
number — it's revenue, collections, reliability, and risk all at once. The brief:
**build a single dashboard a regional manager can read in ten seconds and act on.**

## The build

**Headline KPIs with judgment built in.** Beyond raw figures (Revenue **GH₵347.94K**,
**100%** collection rate), I added derived status indicators — *Service Reliability:
Unstable*, *Revenue Performance: Healthy*, and a **Regional Risk Score** — so the
dashboard doesn't just report numbers, it interprets them.

**Regional drill-down.** Outage hours, revenue, and complaints are all broken out by
region, with slicers for region, payment status, and customer type
(commercial / industrial / residential).

## What it shows

- **Average outage hours** range from **6.8h (Ashanti)** down to **4.0h (Eastern)** —
  a clear reliability ranking to target.
- **Revenue by region** is led by **Northern, Western, and Greater Accra**.
- **Complaints by region** don't track revenue — the highest-revenue regions aren't
  the most-complained-about, which reframes where service investment should go.
- A consolidated **risk score** turns several signals into one number leadership can
  watch over time.

## Why it matters

This is the analyst's real job in the public sector: compress messy operational data
into a few honest indicators that drive a decision — where to send a repair crew,
which region's collections to chase, who's at risk.
